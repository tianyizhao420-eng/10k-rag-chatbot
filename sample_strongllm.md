## 1. Do these companies worry about the challenges or business risks in China or India in terms of cloud service?

Yes, the companies mentioned in the text express significant concerns regarding the challenges and business risks associated with operating in China and India, particularly in the context of cloud services. Key points include:

1. **Regulatory Environment**  
   Both China and India have stringent regulations and licensing requirements that can restrict foreign investment and operations in various sectors, including cloud services. The companies are aware that these regulations may change, leading to potential operational disruptions.

2. **Compliance Risks**  
   Strong emphasis on compliance with local laws, including the U.S. Foreign Corrupt Practices Act and other anti-corruption laws. Non-compliance could result in severe penalties, including fines, loss of licenses, or the need to restructure operations.

3. **Operational Challenges**  
   The evolving regulatory landscape in China and India presents uncertainties regarding the interpretation and enforcement of laws. This can create risks for foreign companies trying to navigate these markets, especially if local laws are interpreted differently from expectations.

4. **Market Competition**  
   Local companies in China and India may have a competitive advantage due to their better understanding of local customers and established brand recognition. This intensifies competition for foreign companies in cloud services.

5. **Funding and Contractual Relationships**  
   The ability to access sufficient funding and enforce contractual relationships is critical for cloud operations in China. Any challenges here could hinder business continuity.

6. **Geopolitical Risks**  
   Companies face exposure to geopolitical risks, including political disputes and economic conditions in China and India, which can affect operations and growth strategies.

7. **Cybersecurity Concerns**  
   As companies invest in cloud services, they face increasing cybersecurity threats. The evolving nature of these threats, combined with local regulatory requirements, adds complexity to operating in these markets.

**Conclusion:** Companies are acutely aware of multifaceted risks in China and India, including regulatory compliance, competitive pressures, operational challenges, and geopolitical factors.

## 3. Compared to 2023, does Amazon's liquidity decrease or increase?

### 3.1 Current Assets
- Cash and cash equivalents: 73,387 million (2023) → 78,779 million (2024)  
- Marketable securities: 13,393 million → 22,423 million  
- Inventories: 33,318 million → 34,214 million  
- Accounts receivable, net and other: 52,253 million → 55,451 million  

**Total current assets:** 172,351 million → 190,867 million

### 3.2 Current Liabilities
- Accounts payable: 84,981 million → 94,363 million  
- Accrued expenses and other: 64,709 million → 66,965 million  
- Unearned revenue: 15,227 million → 18,103 million  

**Total current liabilities:** 164,917 million → 179,431 million

### 3.3 Liquidity Analysis
- **Current Ratio (Current Assets / Current Liabilities)**  
  - 2023: 172,351 / 164,917 = 1.04  
  - 2024: 190,867 / 179,431 = 1.06  

**Conclusion:** Amazon's liquidity has **improved** in 2024 compared to 2023.

## 5. Main business segments of Amazon

1. **North America:**  
   Retail operations in the U.S., Canada, and Mexico; online and physical stores; focus on low prices, fast/free delivery, and convenience.

2. **International:**  
   Retail operations outside North America; online and physical stores; broad selection of products and services.

3. **Amazon Web Services (AWS):**  
   Cloud computing services for developers, enterprises, governments, and academic institutions; provides infrastructure, storage, and computing power.

**Additional activities:**  
- **Third-party seller services:** Platforms for external sellers, generating fees  
- **Advertising services:** Solutions for sellers and partners  
- **Subscription services:** Amazon Prime benefits, including fast shipping and streaming content  

**Overall:** Amazon’s business model focuses on e-commerce, cloud computing, and ancillary services that enhance customer experience and revenue.
